Types of dentures

From a full set to a few missing teeth, there's a denture designed for your needs.

Complete Dentures

For patients missing all teeth in the upper, lower or both jaws.

  • Removable and easy to clean
  • Restores function and appearance

Cobalt Chrome Denture

A partial denture with a slim, strong cast-metal framework.

  • Thin, lightweight and very durable
  • Secure clasps and a stable, precise fit

Acrylic Denture

A partial denture on a lightweight pink acrylic base.

  • More budget-friendly
  • Easy to adjust or add more teeth to later

Valplast Dentures

Made from a soft, flexible material that is lightweight and comfortable.

  • Metal-free and more comfortable
  • Aesthetic and durable

Make them last

Caring for your dentures

A little daily care keeps your dentures fresh, comfortable and long-lasting.

  • Clean with a soft brush only.
  • Rinse your dentures after eating.
  • Store them in a denture case when not being worn.
  • Take them out at night to let your gums rest.
  • Visit us regularly for check-ups and relines.
Handle them over a folded towel or a basin of water — dentures can crack if dropped.
