Crown vs BridgeWhat's the difference?

Both restore your smile — but they solve different problems. A crown caps a damaged tooth that's still in place; a bridge fills the gap left by a missing one. Here's how to tell them apart.

What they are

A crown covers — a bridge replaces. Almost every other difference follows from that one idea.

Dental Crown

A protective cap

A crown is a custom "cap" that covers and protects a damaged or weakened tooth — restoring its strength, shape and appearance.

Used for
  • Protecting a weak tooth from breaking
  • Restoring a broken, cracked or worn-down tooth
  • Covering a tooth after a root canal
  • Improving the shape, size or appearance of a tooth
Key points
  • Covers one existing tooth
  • Restores and protects
  • Does not replace missing teeth

Dental Bridge

Fills the gap

A bridge replaces one or more missing teeth by anchoring an artificial tooth — the "pontic" — to the natural teeth on either side of the gap.

Used for
  • Replacing one or more missing teeth
  • Restoring your smile and ability to chew
  • Preventing remaining teeth from shifting out of position
  • Maintaining the natural shape of your face
Key points
  • Replaces one or more missing teeth
  • Anchored by the teeth on both sides
  • Fixed in place — not removable like dentures

How each one works

Both start by gently reshaping teeth — but a crown restores one tooth, while a bridge spans a gap using its neighbours.

The gap is filled with a complete, natural-looking smile.

Which one

Which is right for you?

It usually comes down to one question: is the tooth still there, or is it missing?

Your tooth is damaged but still there

If a tooth is cracked, worn or weakened — but its root is healthy — a crown caps and protects it. Think of it as a helmet for a tooth.

You have a gap from a missing tooth

If one or more teeth are missing and you have healthy teeth on both sides of the gap, a bridge can fill it and restore your bite.

Curious about other options?

An implant replaces a missing tooth root and all; dentures are removable. Your dentist will help you weigh comfort, cost and longevity.
