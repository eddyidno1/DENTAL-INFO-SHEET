Dental ImplantsRestore your smile with lasting strength.

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone to replace a missing tooth root. It acts as a strong foundation for a crown that looks, feels and functions like a natural tooth.

The building blocks

Anatomy of a dental implant

Every implant is built from three parts that work together — anchored by a titanium root that fuses with your own bone.

Crown · visible part

Osseointegration · the science

Over 3–6 months the titanium fuses with your jaw bone, becoming remarkably strong and stable — and helping prevent the bone loss that follows a missing tooth.

Make it last

Caring for your implants

Implants can't decay, but the gum and bone around them still need looking after. Treat them like natural teeth.

  • Clean daily with a soft brush and non-abrasive toothpaste.
  • Floss daily and use interdental cleaners around the crown.
  • Attend regular check-ups and professional cleanings.
  • Avoid smoking and very hard, crunchy foods.
Good oral hygiene = long-lasting results. With proper care, a dental implant can last a lifetime.
Good to know

Frequently asked questions

The things patients ask us most about dental implants.

The procedure is done under local anaesthetic, so you shouldn't feel pain during placement — most patients say it's easier than a tooth extraction. Mild soreness or swelling for a few days afterwards is normal and easily managed.
From placement to final crown, expect roughly 3–9 months. Most of that time is healing — the titanium needs 3–6 months to fully fuse with the bone (osseointegration). The appointments themselves are short.
Most healthy adults with good gum health and enough jawbone are great candidates. If bone has been lost, a bone graft can often rebuild it first. A CBCT 3D scan at your consultation tells us exactly what's possible for you.
An implant can easily last 10+ years, and often longer with good care.
An implant is the only option that replaces the tooth root, so it prevents bone loss and doesn't rely on grinding down neighbouring teeth (as a bridge does) or removable plates (like a denture). It's the most natural, self-supporting and long-lasting choice.
